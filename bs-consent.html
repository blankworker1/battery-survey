Battery Survey — Pre-Harvest Consent

Vehicle Diagnostic Test Agreement

1. What is about to happen

A Battery Survey harvester is about to connect a Bluetooth OBD2 diagnostic device to the OBD port of your vehicle. Using an approved read-only diagnostic application, they will read battery health data from your vehicle's Battery Management System and screen-record the results.

The data collected will be submitted to the Battery Survey open database at batterysurvey.info, where it will be published permanently under a Creative Commons licence and may be accessed by any member of the public.

3. What data will be collected

  • Battery State of Health (SOH) percentage
  • Individual cell voltages and cell balance (delta)
  • Battery temperature readings
  • Cumulative charge counts — rapid DC and slow AC
  • BMS reprogramming history (Renault vehicles)
  • Pack voltage and amp-hour capacity
  • Odometer reading
  • Vehicle Identification Number (VIN) as read from BMS

Your name and personal details are not published. The record is associated with the vehicle registration number only.

4. Pre-existing fault codes

Important — please read carefully Your vehicle may contain stored fault codes in its onboard computer that are not currently displayed on the dashboard. Connecting a diagnostic device sometimes causes the vehicle to display these codes as warning lights. If this happens, it indicates a pre-existing condition in your vehicle — the diagnostic device cannot create new faults. Battery Survey and the harvester accept no liability for pre-existing vehicle faults that become visible during testing.

5. Limitation of liability

Battery Survey is a data collection service. The battery health record produced is a point-in-time snapshot of data read from your vehicle's own systems. It is not a warranty, guarantee, mechanical inspection, or professional assessment. Neither Battery Survey nor the harvester accepts liability for any decisions made based on this data.

The harvester is using approved, read-only hardware as specified in clause 2. In the unlikely event of damage caused directly by negligent use of unapproved hardware, liability rests with the harvester personally, not with Battery Survey.
