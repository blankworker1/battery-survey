The role

What a harvester actually does

A harvester collects standardised battery health data from a supported electric vehicle using a free diagnostic app and a small Bluetooth dongle. The data goes into the Battery Survey database — a permanent, public, openly licensed record that anyone can look up.

There are two ways to harvest. You can harvest your own vehicle — plug in, run the app, record the screen, submit the data. Or you can travel to another vehicle at someone else's request — a private buyer wanting an independent check before purchase, a community fleet needing a periodic record, a seller wanting to add a verified badge to their listing.

Either way, you are not diagnosing the vehicle, giving advice, or making recommendations. You are collecting and submitting a standardised set of data from the vehicle's own computer. The data is the product.

No technical background needed The harvester manual walks you through every step with photos and plain English instructions. If you can follow a recipe, you can run a battery harvest. Your first test will take longer while you get familiar. By your third test it will feel routine.
Already own a Leaf, Zoe, or Kangoo ZE? You are already most of the way there. If you have a compatible OBD dongle — a Konnwei KW902, ELM327 v1.5, or similar — you can harvest your own vehicle right now. No travel, no coordination with a seller, no waiting for a request. Plug in, record the screen, submit the data, earn £15. Your own car becomes the first entry in your harvester profile, and every vehicle you've owned or have access to is a potential record. The UK has tens of thousands of supported EVs on the road — many of them owned by people who have no idea their battery data could contribute to a public good.
Requirements

What you need

Required 📱
Android smartphone

Android 6.0 or later. The diagnostic apps used by Battery Survey are Android-only. iPhone is not supported at this stage.

Required 🔌
OBD2 Bluetooth dongle

A Konnwei KW902 — about £10–15 on Amazon or eBay. This is the only hardware you need to buy. It plugs into the vehicle's diagnostic port.

Required 🚶
Ability to travel locally

Most harvests are within 10–20 miles of where you live. You don't need a car — train, bus, or bike works fine for most locations.

Required 📋
Reliability and honesty

The entire value of Battery Survey depends on accurate, unmanipulated data. Harvesters who submit clean, complete records build reputation over time.

Helpful 🚗
Your own transport

Useful for rural harvests or pre-purchase inspections where the vehicle can't be driven. Not required for most urban and suburban jobs.

Helpful ⚡
EV knowledge or interest

Not required — but if you already know Leafs and Zoes, you'll hit the ground running. Many harvesters are EV owners themselves.

The hardware — £10–15, once

You need one dongle. That's it. The same dongle works for every Renault ZE vehicle on the Battery Survey supported list. For Nissan Leaf you'll need a second compatible ELM327 dongle — also around £15.

🔌
Konnwei KW902 — Bluetooth OBD2

ELM327 v1.5 protocol, Bluetooth 3.0, Android compatible. The one the Renault ZE community has tested and confirmed works reliably with CanZE. No app lock-in — connects to any approved diagnostic app.

Find on Amazon → approx. £10–15
What to avoid Do not buy a dongle with a dedicated proprietary app (Veepeak, FIXD, etc.). These lock the connection to their own software and won't work with the Battery Survey diagnostic apps. Look specifically for "Konnwei KW902" or "ELM327 v1.5 Bluetooth" without a named companion app.

The process

How a harvest works

1
You receive a harvest request
Someone — a private buyer, a community fleet manager, a seller wanting to add a verified badge — requests a test for a specific vehicle in your area. You get the registration, the address, the vehicle details, and a agreed fee. You confirm you can do it.
Each harvest
2
Travel to the vehicle
Most harvests are at a private address, a dealer forecourt, or a community vehicle depot. The vehicle owner or seller is usually present. You introduce yourself, explain what you're doing in a sentence, and get started.
Each harvest
3
Plug in, connect, record
Plug the dongle under the dashboard. Turn the ignition to ready mode. Open the app, connect, navigate to the battery screens. Start screen recording on your phone. Read the key values out loud on camera — SOH, cell delta, charge counts. Scroll through the full cell grid slowly. Stop recording. The whole process takes about 10 minutes at the vehicle.
Each harvest
4
Submit the data
Fill in the Battery Survey submission form — the key readings, the vehicle details, any notes. Upload the screen recording as evidence. Submit. The record is reviewed and published to the database, usually within a few hours.
Each harvest
5
Get paid
Payment is confirmed once the record is accepted as complete. Bank transfer, PayPal, or whatever works. No invoicing required — Battery Survey handles the payment on confirmation.
Each harvest
