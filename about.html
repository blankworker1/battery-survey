About Battery Survey

The missing data layer
for electric vehicles

Battery Survey is an open, community-built database of electric vehicle battery health records. Independent, transparent, and free at the point of access.

How it started

A van in Birkenhead

Battery Survey started with one question: how do you know if a used electric vehicle's battery is actually healthy before you buy it?

The answer, for most people, was that you didn't. You could look at the dashboard bars, scroll through the seller's description, or pay £150–500 for a mechanic who probably didn't understand the data any better than you did. The diagnostic tools existed — open source apps that read directly from the vehicle's own computer — but no one had built the infrastructure to make them usable by ordinary people at scale.

"The data exists inside every electric vehicle. It just needs someone to collect it honestly and publish it openly."

The proof of concept was a 2017 Renault Kangoo ZE in Birkenhead — battery record BS-2026-00001. A £15 Bluetooth dongle, a free app, a screen recording, and a community project in North Wales that needed to buy a reliable electric van without being able to physically inspect it. That first harvest proved the model worked.

Battery Survey grew from that single record. The goal is simple: every electric vehicle in the UK should have an independent, publicly accessible battery health history — the same way every vehicle has an MOT history.

What we believe

The principles

01
The data belongs to the public
Battery health information read from a vehicle's own computer is not proprietary. It belongs to the owner and to anyone with a legitimate reason to understand it. Battery Survey publishes this data openly under a Creative Commons licence. Anyone can download, analyse, or build on it.
02
No record is ever deleted for commercial reasons
A battery in poor condition earns the same record as one in excellent condition. No seller, dealer, or fleet operator can request suppression of a valid test result. Records are only ever voided if they are technically invalid — wrong vehicle, failed connection, submission error. The integrity of the database is unconditional.
03
The methodology is published openly
The Battery Survey data standard is a public document. Every field, every validation rule, every threshold is documented and open to scrutiny. Anyone can challenge a result, propose a change, or audit the process. There is no black box.
04
Community trust is built slowly and carefully
Battery Survey started with one vehicle and one harvester. The database grows through real tests, real harvesters, and real community organisations. We will not claim scale we don't have, accuracy we can't demonstrate, or authority we haven't earned.
05
The tools are open source and free
Battery Survey uses CanZE, LeafSpy, and other community-built diagnostic apps — tools that have been validated by thousands of EV owners over years. We don't lock data behind proprietary hardware. A £15 dongle and a free app should give any individual access to the same quality of battery information as a main dealer.

Users

Who Battery Survey is for

🛒
Used EV buyers

Anyone buying a second-hand electric vehicle who wants independent battery data before committing. The free lookup answers the basic question. The premium record answers the detailed one.

🏘️
Community transport operators

CICs, charities, and council-funded transport schemes operating electric fleets. Regular six-monthly checks provide a documented battery health record for trustees and funders.

🛡️
Insurers

EV insurance is currently priced on age and mileage. Battery Survey provides the actual battery condition data needed for genuine risk-based pricing — updated every six months.

🔬
Researchers

The Battery Survey dataset is open and queryable. Real-world UK EV battery degradation data, including charge history and temperature records, available under CC BY 4.0.

💼
Small fleet operators

Tradespeople, delivery businesses, and small enterprises running electric vans. A Battery Survey record at purchase and every six months is a legitimate business expense and responsible fleet management.

📋
Sellers and dealers

Any seller who wants to demonstrate their vehicle's battery health honestly and increase buyer confidence. A Battery Survey record on a listing is free to link and transparent to verify.

The standard

An open standard, not a product

The long-term goal of Battery Survey is not to be a company that sells data. It is to establish a standard — the way the MOT is a standard, or the Land Registry is a standard — that the entire EV ecosystem can rely on.

The BS Record Format is published openly. Any developer, researcher, or organisation can implement it. Any harvester can submit to it. Any insurer, lender, or fleet manager can query it. The standard belongs to the community, not to Battery Survey Ltd.

When the dataset has sufficient scale and the methodology has sufficient credibility, the right home for Battery Survey's database is probably not a private company at all — it's a public interest organisation, a cooperative, or a regulated data trust. That conversation is for the future. For now, the work is to build the data.

Contribute to the standard The Battery Survey data standard is hosted on GitHub. If you have technical knowledge of EV diagnostics, fleet management, or battery chemistry and want to contribute, open an issue or submit a pull request. The standard improves when more people look at it.
