01

Purpose and scope

A Battery Survey record is a standardised, timestamped snapshot of an electric vehicle battery's condition, collected by a verified harvester using approved open-source diagnostic tools. It is the most complete publicly available record of an individual EV battery's condition in the UK — capturing every data field the vehicle's own BMS exposes, automatically enriched with GOV.UK vehicle and MOT history data at the moment of submission.

The standard is intentionally exhaustive at the data collection layer and deliberately minimal at the interpretation layer. It captures everything the hardware can read. It does not invent scores, apply weightings, or generate proprietary ratings. The raw data is the product.

Individual buyers

Full BMS snapshot including charge history — data no visual inspection or mileage check can reveal. Automatically cross-referenced against MOT history.

Insurers

Periodic standardised records including rapid charge counts, cell balance, and temperature history — enabling genuine risk-based battery pricing.

Community fleets

Longitudinal record of fleet battery health over time, with GOV.UK MOT data integrated, for trustee and funder reporting.

Researchers

Open, queryable dataset including fields no other public dataset captures — charge cycle counts, BMS reprogramming history, multi-sensor temperatures.

Record identifier

Every Battery Survey record is assigned a unique, permanent, human-readable identifier at submission. This identifier appears on the certificate, in the database, and in all API responses.

D — Charge history  new group v1.1
Why charge history matters The number of rapid DC charges is invisible to any visual inspection, any mileage check, and any competing certificate product. A Leaf with 800 rapid charges tells a fundamentally different story to one with 80 rapid charges at the same mileage. This data is read directly from the BMS and cannot be reset or manipulated by the seller.
rapid_charge_count new
Integer
Required Total number of DC rapid charge events recorded by BMS since manufacture. Cannot be reset.e.g. 247
slow_charge_count new
Integer
Required Total number of AC Level 1/Level 2 charge events recorded by BMS.e.g. 1842
rapid_charge_ratio new
Decimal
Auto Calculated on submission. Rapid charges as a percentage of total charge events. High ratios on older passive-cooled vehicles correlate with faster degradation.e.g. 11.8
E — BMS history  new group v1.1
BMS manipulation detection Renault vehicles are known to show inflated SOH figures after dealer software updates. The BMS reprogramming count and date fields allow Battery Survey to flag records where a recent BMS update may have affected the reported SOH. This is the only publicly documented method of detecting this issue at point of test.

04

GOV.UK enrichment data

On submission, Battery Survey automatically queries two GOV.UK APIs using the vehicle registration number. The returned data is stored as a separate sub-object within the record. The harvester does nothing — this enrichment is entirely automatic. Both APIs are free, open government licence, and JSON-native.

Vehicle Enquiry Service
DVLA · driver-vehicle-licensing.api.gov.uk

Returns make, model, colour, fuel type, month of first registration, current MOT status, tax status, CO2 emissions, and revenue weight. Confirms vehicle identity independently of the seller.

MOT History API
DVSA · history.mot.api.gov.uk

Returns full MOT history including test dates, pass/fail results, mileage at each test, reasons for failure, and advisory notices. Covers all GB vehicles since 2005.

Odometer cross-check The mileage recorded at each MOT is compared against the odometer reading submitted by the harvester. A discrepancy — for example, an odometer reading lower than the most recent MOT mileage — auto-applies the odometer_anomaly flag. This is a tamper detection mechanism that no competing product offers.

05

Battery grade thresholds

A five-grade scale applied automatically on submission. The grade is a convenience label — raw data is always published alongside it. Grades cannot be manually overridden.

Important The grade reflects condition at time of test only. It is not a prediction of future performance, a warranty, or a safety certification. Always read the full record, not just the grade.
GradeLabelSOH %Cell deltaWhat it means
AExcellent90%+Under 30 mVOutstanding condition. Cells well balanced. Performs close to manufacturer specification.
BGood80–89%Under 50 mVNormal age-related degradation. Performing well. Suitable for most use cases.
CFair70–79%Under 80 mVModerate degradation. Functional but range noticeably reduced. Price should reflect condition.
DPoor60–69%Under 120 mVSignificant degradation. Suitable only for short-range, low-demand use.
FFailBelow 60%120 mV+Severely degraded or faulty cell. Specialist assessment recommended before purchase or continued fleet use.
?UngradedN/AN/AConnection failed or data incomplete. Record retained. Retest recommended.
Delta override rule If cell delta exceeds the threshold for a given SOH grade, the grade is lowered by one level. For example SOH of 85% is normally Grade B, but a delta of 75mV lowers it to Grade C. Always transparent in the published record.
High rapid charge ratio flag If rapid_charge_ratio exceeds 25% on a passive-cooled vehicle (Nissan Leaf 24kWh or 30kWh), the flag high_rapid_charge_ratio is auto-applied regardless of grade. This does not change the grade but is prominently displayed on the record page.

08

Data integrity rules

Validation rules
Registration
Format
Must match UK plate format.
VIN
Format
17 characters, ISO 3779. Must match DVLA-returned VIN where available. Mismatch auto-applies vin_mismatch flag.
SOH range
Range
0.0–100.0. Values above 100.0 held for review — likely BMS fault or dongle incompatibility.
Cell delta
Range
Positive integer. Above 500mV flagged for review.
Cell voltages
Range
2.500–4.300V. Min ≤ max. Delta between min and max must equal cell_delta_mv ±5mV.
Odometer check
Logic
Compared against gov_mot_last_mileage_km. If odometer_km is lower than last MOT mileage, odometer_anomaly flag auto-applied.
BMS reprogram
Logic
If bms_reprogram_last_date is within 90 days of test_date, bms_update_suspected flag auto-applied.
Rapid charge ratio
Logic
If rapid_charge_ratio > 25% on passive-cooled vehicle, high_rapid_charge_ratio flag auto-applied.
Evidence URL
Presence
Must be accessible at submission. Dead links cause rejection.
Duplicate check
Logic
Same registration within 30 days triggers warning but not rejection.
On transparency Battery Survey publishes the raw data. Records can only be voided if technically invalid — not because the result is inconvenient. No seller, dealer, or fleet operator can request suppression of a valid record.
